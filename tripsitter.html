Market Research

The Stats

Approximately 1.5 billion people take vacation trips yearly. More than 75% of people use a smartphone app to plan and book their trips generating 798 billion dollars annually in revenue.

The Problem

One of most stressful parts of traveling is actually planning the trip itself. While there are many apps specifically used for booking flights, accommodations and activities, very few combine these travel details in one easily accessible app.

Competitor Analysis

I analyzed 3 top travel apps in the space- looking at how these apps organized travel plans and how they connect users to traveling resources.

The Pros

Most apps have the ability to search for housing and accommodations. They allow you to save content for later reference.

The Cons

Lack interface to remind you of events during a trip and create a unified itinerary. Do not allow you to manually enter activity information. Navigation and organization are confusing.

Study Results

Even though most users were able to complete the tasks, there were certain design elements that could be modified with better usability throughout the app. The main focus of the reiteration would be connecting different tasks within the app at key screens with navigational tabs. There were also small changes that would help interacting with certain elements more accessible. .

High-Fidelity Style Guide

After reiterating components of the app with feedback from user testing, I began to define the main colors, fonts and other characteristics of the app into a style guide. I chose the the colors because they promote a relaxing feel with a hint of vibrancy.

Project Reflection

Much like this project, the process of traveling has many challenges but the destination is always worth it! This project had a large scope in terms of defining what problem users face with regards to travel and the best way to go about improving it. Since there are so many variables when traveling, the biggest challenge was defining and ideating the most important features that would help users. Spending the time, effort and energy to produce a high fidelity prototype with clearly defined features resulted in a more insightful user testing experience. It is invaluable to be able to pinpoint the pain points of users with a high fidelity iterations and be able to improve specific parts. With feedback from users and utilizing foundational UX design and thinking, I developed a mock product that I believe could be a start to solve a real world problem and improve the experience of travel.
